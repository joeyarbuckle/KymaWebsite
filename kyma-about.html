The Founding Philosophy

Built for the
landowner's side of the table.

Kyma Partners was founded on a straightforward conviction: landowners have been the least-compensated participants in the development equation for too long — contributing the most fundamental ingredient and receiving the smallest share of the value created.

The firm brings together two principals with complementary and deeply practical expertise: one who has spent three decades structuring real estate investment transactions across institutional and family office contexts, and one who has spent 25 years turning raw land into entitled, engineered, development-ready sites.

Kyma participates as a principal and co-investor in every transaction — not as a broker or intermediary. The firm's return depends on the same outcome as the landowner's: a successfully completed, cash-flowing asset.

"The landowner has been the least-compensated participant in the development equation for too long — contributing the most fundamental ingredient and receiving the smallest share of the value created."

Independence of Valuation

Land is contributed at appraised market value established by an independent MAI-designated appraiser — not a number set by the developer.

No Capital Required of the Landowner

The landowner's contribution is the land itself. Kyma and its development partners provide all cash equity and carry all construction loan guarantees.

Genuine Choice at the Finish Line

At stabilization, the landowner elects their outcome — permanent equity with quarterly distributions, or a cash buyout. Both paths are structured to deliver fair market value.

Alignment Through Co-Investment

Kyma participates as a principal in each transaction. The firm's return depends on the same outcome as the landowner's — a successfully completed, cash-flowing asset.

Joel Arbuckle

Co-Founder  ·  MBA, Northwestern University — Kellogg

Joel brings nearly 30 years of institutional real estate investment experience spanning acquisitions, development, portfolio and asset management, equity joint ventures, dispositions, and structured finance across multiple sectors — multifamily, industrial, office, retail, storage, hospitality, and agriculture. His career has taken him from large national advisory firms to boutique operators and family office platforms, with a consistent focus on identifying sound investment opportunities and structuring transactions that create long-term value for all parties.

At Kyma, Joel leads deal sourcing, joint venture structuring, LP capital formation, and investor relations. He participates in every transaction as a principal — not as a broker — ensuring his interests are directly aligned with those of the landowner.
